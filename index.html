Current document management systems are riddled with problems that cause headaches and waste hours of valuable time

Fragmentation of document management sources & libraries

Important and valuable documents, information and data spread across multiple repositories including individual email accounts, individual computers, folders, hierarchy-driven shared drives, and even paper filing cabinets.

Difficult discoverability

When information is shared across a wide range of repositories, searching and filtering is never a simple process, and often requires remembering complex paths within folder structures.

Unsecure administration & access

It can be quite difficult to assign permissions and determine proper access levels across organizations within the current set of solutions.

Lack of document curation

Most organizations have thousands to millions of old documents that are no longer valuable, having been stored over years or decades. Due to space and time constraints, users often find it difficult to determine what to upload and share, and therefore opt to instead not store or share at all.

Inefficient file-sharing

When sharing information across the organization, resorting to compressing enormous files and emailing them around is not the best use of time or server storage space.

Feeling stuck

Document management systems can be complex to implement, leaving many organizations feeling “stuck” with their current solution, even if it lacks the robust functionality and efficiency / effectiveness the organization desires.

Lost Productivity $27K per year

To maintain their company knowledge, the investment office waists 14% (4000 hours) of their time filing, tagging, and naming files in nested folders each year.

Inbox Full
